# Market Trends & Insights

## Overview

This section analyzes key market trends shaping the London AIML job market, including the impact of generative AI, demand-supply dynamics, and future projections.

---

## 1. The GenAI Revolution (2023-2026)

### Explosive Job Growth

| Period | AI Jobs in London | % of All IT Jobs | YoY Growth |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|------------|
| **Mar 2024** | 935 | 4.40% | Baseline |
| **Mar 2025** | 1,556 | 6.97% | +66.4% |
| **Mar 2026** | 2,395 | 14.03% | +53.9% |

**Key Insight**: AI job postings have **more than doubled** in 24 months, with AI now representing **1 in 7 IT jobs** in London.

### GenAI-Specific Roles

| Role | 2024 | 2025 | 2026 | Growth |
|------|------|------|------|--------|
| **LLM Engineer** | Rare | 150+ | 400+ | 167% |
| **Prompt Engineer** | <50 | 200+ | 350+ | 75% |
| **AI Product Manager** | 100+ | 250+ | 450+ | 80% |
| **GenAI Researcher** | 50+ | 150+ | 300+ | 100% |

### Salary Inflation (Then Correction)

| Period | Median AI Salary | Change |
|--------|------------------|--------|
| **2023** | £75,000 | Baseline |
| **2024** | £85,000 | +13.3% |
| **2025** | £92,500 | +8.8% |
| **2026** | £81,500 | -11.9% |

**Analysis**: The 2026 "correction" reflects market maturation rather than declining demand. Early GenAI roles commanded unsustainable premiums.

## 9. Key Insights Summary

### For Job Seekers

1. **GenAI skills are table stakes** - Not optional for 2026+
2. **Specialize deeply** - Generalists face commoditization
3. **MLOps = Job Security** - Deployment skills always needed
4. **Domain expertise matters** - Combine AI + Industry knowledge

### For Employers

1. **Talent war continues** - Expect 10-15% annual salary inflation
2. **Retention > Hiring** - Replacing talent costs 150%+ of salary
3. **Remote is expected** - Hybrid flexibility minimum
4. **Skills > Degrees** - Portfolio > pedigree

### For Policymakers

1. **Skills gap threatens competitiveness** - Education reform needed
2. **Immigration essential** - 30% of senior roles filled internationally
3. **Regulatory clarity** - Uncertainty slows investment
4. **Regional balance** - London concentration risks
