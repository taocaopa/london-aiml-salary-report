# Industry Comparison: AIML vs Other Sectors

## Overview

This section compares AIML role salaries against other technology and professional sectors to provide context on the competitive positioning of AI and Machine Learning careers in London.

---

## 1. AIML vs General Software Engineering

### Salary Comparison (London, Median Annual GBP)

| Role | Median Salary | Premium vs SWE |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| AI Engineer | £81,500 | +9.4% |
| Data Scientist | £75,000 | +0.7% |
| ML Engineer | £67,250 | -9.7% |
| Software Engineer | £74,500 | baseline |

### Key Insights

- **AI Engineers** command a modest premium over general Software Engineers, reflecting the specialized nature of AI development
- **Data Scientists** earn roughly equivalent to Software Engineers at the median level
- **ML Engineers** show slightly lower median salaries, potentially due to:
  - Sample composition effects (more junior roles in dataset)
  - Geographic distribution (more UK-wide roles vs London-centric)
  - Hybrid nature of role combining SWE and Data Science responsibilities

### Total Compensation Differential

When including bonuses and equity:
- **Big Tech AIML roles**: 20-40% premium over general SWE
- **Finance AIML roles**: 30-60% premium over general SWE
- **Startup AIML roles**: Equity upside can far exceed SWE equivalents

## 8. Key Takeaways

1. **Finance pays most**: AIML roles in finance offer 50-100% higher total compensation than tech
2. **Big Tech offers balance**: Competitive base + significant equity upside
3. **Healthcare offers purpose**: Lower pay offset by mission alignment and stability
4. **Geography matters**: London commands significant premiums over other UK locations
5. **Hybrid skills valued**: MLOps and AI Engineering roles bridge SWE and Data Science
6. **GenAI premium**: LLM/Generative AI specialists command 20-30% premiums
