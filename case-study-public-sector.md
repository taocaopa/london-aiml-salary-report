# Case Study: Public Sector AI Leadership

---

## Position Overview

**Role**: Director General for Emerging Technology & Artificial Intelligence  
**Department**: Department for Science, Innovation & Technology (DSIT)  
**Salary**: £174,000 per annum  
**Pension**: £50,407 employer contribution (28.97% of salary)  
**Total Package Value**: £224,407  
**Locations**: Darlington, London, Manchester  
**Reports to**: Permanent Secretary / Ministers

## Career Context

### Civil Service Progression

| Grade | Typical Salary | Scope |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| **SEO/Grade 7** | £60,000-75,000 | Team lead |
| **HEO/Grade 6** | £75,000-95,000 | Unit head |
| **SCS1** | £95,000-125,000 | Director |
| **SCS2** | £125,000-165,000 | DG level |
| **SCS3** | £165,000-200,000 | Perm Sec potential |

**This role is SCS2/SCS3 level**, representing the senior-most tier of civil service leadership.

### Comparison: Private Sector Path to Equivalent

**Typical Private Sector Journey:**
- Years 0-5: IC contributor to Senior Engineer (£45k-£85k)
- Years 5-8: Staff/Principal Engineer (£90k-£140k)
- Years 8-12: Director of Engineering/AI (£150k-£200k)
- Years 12+: VP/CTO level (£200k-£500k+)

**Total Time to Equivalent**: 12-15 years

### Unique Aspects of Public Sector Role

**Advantages:**
- **Mission Impact**: Shape national AI strategy
- **Scale**: Affect millions of citizens
- **Stability**: No layoff risk
- **Pension**: Defined benefit security
- **Visibility**: Public leadership platform

**Challenges:**
- **Bureaucracy**: Complex approval processes
- **Politics**: Ministerial and media scrutiny
- **Budget Constraints**: Limited vs private sector
- **Speed**: Slower decision-making
- **Compensation Ceiling**: Fixed pay scales

## Recruitment Context

### The Challenge

The UK government faces intense competition for AI talent:
- **DeepMind (London)** hires top PhDs at £150k-300k+
- **US Tech Giants** offer £200k-400k for London roles
- **Hedge Funds** pay £300k-800k for quant ML roles

### Civil Service Response

1. **Competitive Salaries**: £174k exceeds 99% of civil service roles
2. **Flexible Location**: Darlington, London, Manchester options
3. **Mission Appeal**: "AI for public good" narrative
4. **Career Progression**: Path to Permanent Secretary

### Candidate Profile

**Ideal Candidate Likely Has:**
- **15+ years** technology leadership experience
- **Mixed background**: Private sector + government/academia
- **AI expertise**: Technical depth + policy understanding
- **Stakeholder skills**: Ministerial/board-level exposure
- **Track record**: Delivering at-scale technology programmes

## Key Takeaways

### For Job Seekers

1. **Public sector pays competitively** at senior AI levels
2. **Pension benefits** can offset lower base vs private sector
3. **Mission-driven work** offers non-monetary satisfaction
4. **Career ceiling** exists in civil service pay bands

### For Policymakers

1. **Salary investment** necessary to attract AI talent
2. **Competition is global** - talent is mobile
3. **Mission matters** - not everyone maximizes income
4. **Skills shortage** requires creative recruitment

### For Researchers

1. **Public sector increasingly important** AI employer
2. **Compensation converging** with private sector at top levels
3. **Mission alignment** attracts different talent pool
4. **Regulatory expertise** increasingly valuable
