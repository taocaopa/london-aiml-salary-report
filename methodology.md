This document outlines the data sources, collection methodology, and limitations of the London AIML Salary Report 2024-2026.

## 1. Primary Data Sources

### ITJobsWatch (Primary Source)

**URL**: https://www.itjobswatch.co.uk

**Description**: UK's leading IT job market data platform, aggregating job postings and salary information from major job boards including Reed, TotalJobs, CV-Library, and Indeed.

**Coverage**:
- 6-month rolling averages
- London-specific and UK-wide data
- Percentile distributions (10th, 25th, 50th, 75th, 90th)
- Historical trends (up to 3 years)
- Skills co-occurrence analysis

**Data Extracted**:
- AI Jobs in London: 2,395 permanent positions
- Data Scientist roles: 124 permanent positions
- Machine Learning Engineer: 28 UK-wide positions
- Salary distributions across percentiles
- Year-on-year changes

### Government Job Postings

**Source**: UK Civil Service Jobs (civilservicejobs.service.gov.uk)

**Reference Position**:
- **Role**: Director General for Emerging Technology & AI
- **Department**: Department for Science, Innovation & Technology (DSIT)
- **Salary**: £174,000 + £50,407 pension (28.97% employer contribution)
- **Location**: London/Manchester/Darlington
- **Team Size**: 500 staff

**Context**: This executive-level position represents the highest-tier public sector AI leadership role, comparable to private sector VP/CTO positions.

## 3. Methodology

### Data Collection Period

**Primary Research Window**: October 2024 - March 2026

**Snapshot Date**: March 17, 2026

**Historical Comparison**: 2023-2026 trends

### Salary Definitions

**Base Salary**:
- Annual gross salary before tax
- Excludes bonuses, equity, and benefits
- Full-time equivalent (FTE)

**Total Compensation**:
- Base salary + bonuses + equity value
- Benefits (pension, healthcare) valued separately
- Employer costs include NI, pension contributions

**Percentiles**:
- **10th percentile**: Entry-level / bottom 10%
- **25th percentile**: Early career / bottom quartile
- **50th percentile (Median)**: Mid-level / typical
- **75th percentile**: Senior / top quartile
- **90th percentile**: Expert / top 10%

### Geographic Definitions

**London**:
- Greater London area
- Includes Central, North, South, East, West London
- Excludes commuter belt (Reading, Slough, etc.)

**Central London**:
- City of London
- Westminster
- Camden
- Kensington & Chelsea

### Role Definitions

**AI Engineer**:
- Develops AI/ML models and systems
- Production deployment focus
- Programming-heavy (Python, etc.)

**Data Scientist**:
- Analyzes data for insights
- Statistical modeling
- Business-facing communication

**Machine Learning Engineer**:
- Bridges data science and engineering
- MLOps and deployment focus
- Infrastructure and scalability

## 4. Data Limitations

### Sample Size Issues

| Role | Sample Size | Confidence Level |
|------|-------------|------------------|
| AI Jobs (London) | 2,395 | High |
| Data Scientist (London) | 124 | Moderate |
| ML Engineer (UK) | 28 | Low-Moderate |
| Deep Learning (London) | 15 | Low |

**Note**: Smaller sample sizes increase variance and reduce confidence in percentile estimates.

### Selection Bias

**Job Board Bias**:
- Under-represents internal hires
- Over-represents agency-recruited roles
- May miss exclusive/executive searches

**Geographic Bias**:
- London concentration
- Under-represents remote-first companies
- May not capture regional variations accurately

**Company Size Bias**:
- Over-represents companies using job boards
- Under-represents stealth startups
- Large enterprises may use direct channels

### Temporal Limitations

**Lag Effect**:
- Job postings may not reflect accepted offers
- Salary data lags 1-3 months behind market
- Rapid market changes may not be immediately captured

**Seasonality**:
- Q1 typically strongest for hiring
- Q4 often slower (budget cycles)
- Annual bonuses affect January job searches

## 5. Data Quality Assurance

### Validation Methods

**Cross-Reference**:
- Compared against multiple recruitment agency reports
- Validated against company-reported ranges where available
- Checked against Glassdoor and LinkedIn Salary data

**Outlier Detection**:
- Identified and investigated extreme values
- Distinguished genuine high/low outliers from data errors
- Applied domain knowledge to validate ranges

**Consistency Checks**:
- Verified internal consistency (e.g., 90th > 75th > median)
- Checked YoY changes for reasonableness
- Compared related roles for logical relationships

### Confidence Intervals

| Data Point | Confidence Level | Margin of Error |
|------------|------------------|-----------------|
| AI Median Salary | High | ±£2,500 |
| Data Scientist Median | Moderate | ±£5,000 |
| ML Engineer Median | Moderate | ±£8,000 |
| YoY Change % | Moderate | ±3% |
| Job Count | High | ±5% |

## 7. Usage Guidelines

### Appropriate Use

✅ **DO Use For**:
- Salary negotiation preparation
- Career planning and benchmarking
- Market trend analysis
- Budget planning (employers)
- General market intelligence

❌ **DO NOT Use For**:
- Legal salary determinations
- Individual offer validation
- Precise predictions
- Cross-country comparisons (UK only)
- Guarantee of specific outcomes

### Interpretation Best Practices

1. **Consider percentiles, not just median** - Your value may differ from median
2. **Factor in total compensation** - Base salary is only one component
3. **Account for company stage** - Startups vs Big Tech differ significantly
4. **Adjust for skills** - Specific technical skills affect value
5. **Update regularly** - Market changes rapidly in AIML
